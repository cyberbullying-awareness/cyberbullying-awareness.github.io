How to react to Cyberbullying

If you are a victim of Cyberbullying, know firstly that it’s not your fault at all. It’s recommended you notify a friend or family member, trusted adult or someone in authority about the situation so they can help you get through the incident. Ensure that you keep evidence of the incident by screenshotting messages or through another form such as message logs. If needed, meeting with a therapist can also help get through the trauma and help with further incidents.


Preventation of further incidents

To prevent further encounters with the Cyberbully, you should:

1. Block the Cyberbully. Most social media platforms have ways of blocking users. This can come in the form as a “Block” button or “Ignore” button or similar.

2. Report the account of the Cyberbully. Most social media platforms have a report function, where users can report other users for breaking the rules of the platform.

3. Keep evidence as proof that the incident took place.

4. Get assistance from a friend or family member, adult or someone in authority.

5. If the attacks are continous and affect your daily life, it is recommended that you report the incident to the nearest police station or Cybercrime Unit in your country/state. Their role is to provide technical assistance, detection and investigation of the Cybercrime.
