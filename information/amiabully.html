Introduction

If you have sent a mean message to someone, uploaded embarrassing media of someone, said something cruel through voice chat in a video game to someone, you have Cyberbullied. Sometimes Cyberbullying can take place unintentionally due to our emotions as well as misinterpreting of the message.


Help and Preventation

There are many reasons why people Cyberbully, some do it for fun, others do it because they are forced to, some do it as a way to cope with situations at home, school, work or relationships. Remember that it is a real human’s feelings you are dealing with, there can be drastic consequences to your actions.

Some ways to get help if you are a Cyberbully, intentional or unintentional.

1. Talk to a trusted friend, family member or someone in authority. Let them know that you are trying to be less aggressive online.

2. Use your device in a more positive and inspiring way.

3. Think before you send. Think about how your message will affect the person who is on the receiving end. Ask yourself “How would I feel and react if I received this?” or “Will I cause harm onto someone?”.

4. If needed, see a therapist to resolve your issues, be it anger or low self esteem so you can not Cyberbully.
